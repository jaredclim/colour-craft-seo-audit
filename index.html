What this means

  • "Still being indexed" explains some of why microsites aren't fully ranking yet.
  • It doesn't explain the schema placeholder, missing FAQ markup, sitemap structure, or URL typos.
  • Those are build-time defects. Indexation can't resolve them.
  • A capable web team can clear most of them in an afternoon.

Section 04

Critical fixes for Basecoat.

Ordered by impact. Top three are network-wide. Each one fixed at the template level lifts both microsites plus every future franchise.

Fix 01 · Network

Replace the broken schema placeholder.

  • Defect: franchisor homepage JSON-LD contains "telephone":"COMPANYPHONE", a literal placeholder.
  • Fix: rebuild as LocalBusiness with NAP, geo, areaServed, openingHours, priceRange, aggregateRating, sameAs links.
  • Cascade: the broken schema is inherited site-wide. Single fix, network-wide lift.
Critical Low effort High impact Network-wide
Fix 02 · Network

Add schema to every microsite location page.

  • Defect: all 11 neighborhood pages (6 Richmond-Delta + 5 Philadelphia) have zero structured data.
  • Fix: per-page LocalBusiness + Service schema with proper areaServed. Add to franchisor template so future franchises inherit.
  • Why: unlocks local rich results across the entire network at once.
Critical Med effort High impact Network-wide
Fix 03 · Network

Reference both microsite sitemaps in root robots.txt.

  • Defect: root robots.txt only points to the franchisor sitemap. Neither /richmond-delta/sitemap_index.xml nor /philadelphia/sitemap_index.xml is referenced.
  • Fix: add a Sitemap: line for each microsite. Submit each in Google Search Console.
  • Why this matters now: this is the most likely real cause of "still being indexed." Google has to stumble onto the microsites via internal links.
Critical Low effort High impact Network-wide
Fix 04 · Philadelphia

Fix the two URL typos in Philadelphia.

  • Defect: /philadelphia/randor-township-wayne/ should be radnor. /philadelphia/king-of-prussias/ should be king-of-prussia.
  • Risk: typo URLs in major affluent suburbs hurt CTR and brand credibility. Permanent unless renamed soon.
  • Fix: rename slugs, add 301 redirects from old URLs to preserve any link equity.
High Low effort Med impact
Fix 05 · Network

Strengthen franchisor homepage SEO basics.

  • Current: Title "Professional Painting Services | Colour Craft." Meta: "...across multiple markets." H1: "Residential and Commercial."
  • Issue: no service keyword, no location anchor, no compelling click-through copy.
  • Fix: keyword + brand pattern at minimum. e.g. "House Painters Across Canada & the US | Colour Craft Painting."
High Low effort Med impact
Fix 06 · Network

Add FAQ schema + audit page weight.

  • FAQ: location pages already have FAQ-style sections in body copy. Mark them up as FAQPage to unlock "people also ask" placements.
  • Page weight: homepage is 722KB. Run PageSpeed Insights, target sub-2.5-second LCP on mobile.
  • Both: template-level, apply once, cascade everywhere.
Medium Med effort Med impact Network-wide

Suggested sequence

  • Ship first: Fixes 01, 02, 03. Lowest effort, highest leverage, all network-wide.
  • Ship next: Fix 04 (Philadelphia typos) before any link equity builds up on the bad URLs.
  • Queue: Fix 05 (franchisor homepage copy) needs your sign-off. Fix 06 is a 60-day item.

Section 05

Outside Basecoat.

Three items that sit at the franchisor or franchisee level, not the agency.

Item A · Franchisor

Brand-typo domain recovery.

  • colorcraftpainting.com (American spelling) redirects to a third-party link shortener (l.ink).
  • Brand traffic from US-keyboard typing or voice search is being captured by someone outside Colour Craft.
  • Action: acquire the domain and 301-redirect to the Canadian spelling, or pursue legal transfer if it appears to be cybersquatting.
Item B · Coordination

Search Console + GA4 access for franchisees.

  • Franchisees currently can't see which queries their microsites rank for or how the GA4 data is shaping up.
  • Action: have Basecoat grant viewer access to franchisee owners on the GSC + GA4 properties. Costs nothing, unlocks data-driven decisions at the franchisee level.
Item C · Franchisee

Local levers Basecoat can't pull.

  • Google Business Profile completeness (services, products, posts, Q&A, photos).
  • Review velocity. Richmond-Delta is at 49 reviews vs. Makus (Richmond competitor) at 161.
  • Citation NAP consistency across HomeStars, BBB, Yelp, Houzz.
  • Google Local Services Ads enrollment + Google Guaranteed badge.
  • Framing: Basecoat owns the on-site half. Franchisee owns the off-site half. Both move in parallel.
