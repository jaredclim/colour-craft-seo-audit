The content layer Basecoat built is genuinely strong. The technical layer underneath has a handful of fixable defects that are quietly costing rankings, and a couple of items sit outside Basecoat entirely.

This audit was commissioned by Jared to get a clear read on the SEO position of the franchisee site and produce a punch list that's easy to triage. Nothing here is adversarial to Basecoat. Reviewed: live site (colourcraftpainting.com homepage + 6 location pages), schema markup, sitemaps, robots.txt, domain redirects, competitive landscape (Hemlock, Makus, Maple Brothers, Warline, Tall Guy).

Section 01

What Basecoat got right.

Real assets are in place. These are worth protecting and building on.

  • Six dedicated neighborhood pages built and indexed.Steveston, Tsawwassen, Ladner, Richmond, Delta, and the Richmond-Delta hub. All 200-status, all live, all distinct URLs under /richmond-delta/.
  • Content depth is well above local competitors.Each neighborhood page is 6,800 to 7,500 words with localized angles (coastal-ready finishes for Steveston, heritage detail for Ladner). Most competitors run 800 to 1,500 word service pages.
  • On-page basics done correctly at the location level.Unique title tags, unique meta descriptions, unique H1s, and localized neighborhood-naming inside body copy (Cliff Drive, English Bluff, Beach Grove, Broadmoor, Terra Nova).
  • Already ranking #1 organic for "painters Delta BC."The foundation works. The opportunity is to expand that result across every neighborhood in territory, which the technical fixes below directly unlock.

Section 02 · The diagnosis

Strong content layer. Weak technical layer.

The pages read well. What sits behind them in the code is the issue.

In one sentence

The site has the content of a top-three local painter and the technical hygiene of a mid-tier one. Closing that gap is mostly small, well-defined code fixes, not strategic rebuilds.

A few examples of what the technical gap looks like in practice. The homepage structured data (the markup Google uses to understand the business) currently contains the literal placeholder string COMPANYPHONE where the phone number should be. The franchisee neighborhood pages have no structured data at all. The Richmond-Delta microsite has its own sitemap that the root robots.txt does not reference, so Google has to stumble onto it via internal links instead of being told it exists.

None of these are indexation timing issues. They are build-time defects that no amount of waiting will resolve. A capable web team can clear most of them in a single afternoon.

The leverage point is the punch list. Each item below is named specifically enough that Basecoat can scope and ship it without further investigation. The next section is what to send them.

Section 03

Critical fixes for Basecoat.

Ordered by impact. Top three are urgent. Items four through six are high-value but lower-friction.

Fix 01

Replace the broken schema placeholder.

Homepage structured data ships with an unfilled placeholder string. Google sees a broken entity.

The defect
Homepage JSON-LD currently contains "telephone":"COMPANYPHONE". The literal placeholder was never replaced when the template was deployed.
The fix
Replace with full LocalBusiness (or PaintingService) schema including name, address, telephone, geo coordinates, areaServed, openingHours, priceRange, aggregateRating, and sameAs links to Google Business Profile and social accounts.
Why it matters
Eliminates rich-snippet eligibility and entity confidence across every page that inherits the homepage schema. This is the single highest-leverage technical fix on the site.
Severity
CriticalLow effortHigh impact
Fix 02

Add schema to every location page.

Steveston, Tsawwassen, Ladner, Richmond, Delta, Richmond-Delta hub. Zero structured data on any of them.

The defect
All six neighborhood pages have no JSON-LD at all. For a local-intent SEO play, this is the markup that tells Google "this page is about a business serving this place."
The fix
Add per-page LocalBusiness + Service schema with the relevant areaServed values (Steveston, Tsawwassen, etc.) plus FAQ schema on the FAQ-style sections already present in the body copy.
Why it matters
Unlocks rich result eligibility for "people also ask" questions and local pack justifications. The content is already there. It just needs to be marked up.
Severity
CriticalMed effortHigh impact
Fix 03

Make the Richmond-Delta sitemap discoverable.

The microsite has its own sitemap that the root robots.txt doesn't reference. Google has to find it accidentally.

The defect
Root robots.txt only references colourcraftpainting.com/sitemap_index.xml, which lists 27 generic pages. The Richmond-Delta microsite has its own sitemap at /richmond-delta/sitemap_index.xml with 21+ pages plus blog posts, but it's not referenced anywhere Google reliably looks.
The fix
Add a second Sitemap: line to the root robots.txt pointing at the Richmond-Delta sub-sitemap. Then submit both in Google Search Console under the appropriate property.
Why it matters
Without explicit submission, Google's crawl prioritization on the microsite is weaker and slower. This is the most likely real cause behind "still being indexed."
Severity
CriticalLow effortHigh impact
Fix 04

Strengthen the franchisor homepage SEO basics.

Title, meta description, and H1 are generic enough that they actively hurt CTR and rankings.

Current state
Title: "Professional Painting Services | Colour Craft." Meta: "Colour Craft delivers expert residential and commercial painting across multiple markets." H1: "Residential and Commercial."
The issue
No location modifier, no service-specific keyword, no compelling click-through copy. "Across multiple markets" reads like a brochure and tells Google the page is not locally relevant to anyone in particular.
The fix
Even at the franchisor level, swap to keyword + brand patterns: title like "House Painters in BC | Colour Craft Painting," H1 with a service-keyword anchor, meta description naming the value proposition. Franchisee subpages keep their stronger local copy.
Severity
HighLow effortMed impact
Fix 05

Add FAQ schema to the FAQ content already on location pages.

Each neighborhood page has FAQ-style sections in body copy that aren't being marked up as FAQs.

The opportunity
FAQ schema unlocks "people also ask" placements and expanded SERP real estate. Especially valuable for questions like "how much does it cost to paint a house in Richmond BC."
The fix
Wrap existing FAQ content in FAQPage structured data. Same content, just marked up so Google can use it.
Severity
HighLow effortMed impact
Fix 06

Audit page weight and Core Web Vitals.

Homepage is 722KB. Likely hurting mobile rankings and conversion on 4G.

The issue
WordPress + Avada theme bloat. Without seeing Core Web Vitals directly, page weight strongly suggests slow LCP on mobile. Google uses CWV as a ranking factor and a sub-3-second mobile load is table stakes in 2026.
The ask
Run PageSpeed Insights on homepage and each location page. Share results. Prioritize fixes targeting LCP under 2.5 seconds on mobile.
Severity
MediumMed effortMed impact

Suggested sequence

Fixes 01, 02, and 03 are the highest leverage and the lowest effort. They should ship first, ideally together. Fix 04 (homepage basics) needs franchisor sign-off on copy direction. Fix 05 (FAQ schema) is a clean win whenever it gets queued. Fix 06 (page weight) is the longest tail and can be a 60-day item.

Section 04

Outside Basecoat's lane.

Three items that aren't really web-agency work but affect the SEO position. Worth deciding who owns each.

Item A

Brand-typo domain recovery.

colorcraftpainting.com (American spelling) redirects to a third-party link shortener.

What's happening
Anyone who mistypes the domain with the American spelling ends up at colourcraftpainting-com.l.ink/, a redirect controlled by someone other than Colour Craft. Some portion of brand traffic, especially from voice search or US-keyboard typing, is being captured.
The fix
Either acquire the American-spelling domain and 301-redirect it to the Canadian one, or have legal pursue transfer if it appears to be cybersquatting. Brand-protection play, not SEO.
Owner
Franchisor (Brad). Not a Basecoat decision.
Item B

Search Console + Analytics access.

The franchisee can't see actual ranking, impression, or click data on their own subsite.

The gap
Currently no visibility into which queries the Richmond-Delta microsite is ranking on, what the actual impression volume looks like, or whether GA4 is configured correctly for the subsite.
The ask
Basecoat (or whoever owns the GSC + GA4 properties) provides viewer access for the Richmond-Delta franchisee. Costs nothing, unlocks data-driven decision-making at the franchisee level.
Owner
Coordination between Brad, Basecoat, and Jared.
Item C

Local levers Basecoat can't pull.

The franchisee-owned half of SEO that doesn't run through the agency.

What Jared owns
Google Business Profile completeness (services, products, posts, Q&A pre-seed, photos), review velocity (currently 49 reviews vs. Makus at 161), citation NAP consistency across HomeStars, BBB, Yelp, Houzz, Google Local Services Ads enrollment, and the brand-search lift that comes from D2D, yard signs, vans, and community presence.
Why mentioning this
So the framing is clear: Basecoat owns the on-site half, Jared owns the off-site half. Neither can dominate Richmond-Delta on their own. The 12-month picture below assumes both are moving in parallel.
Owner
Franchisee (Jared).

Section 05

The 12-month picture.

With the technical fixes in place and the franchisee-side levers running in parallel, the realistic 12-month state for Richmond & Delta is top-three local pack across every neighborhood in territory, 200+ Google reviews at 4.9, and rankings on every primary keyword from "painters Richmond BC" to "exterior painters Tsawwassen."

The competitive landscape is unusually winnable. Hemlock leads on content but is Vancouver-based. Makus leads on review count but has no warranty and a generic brand. Maple Brothers leads on trust signals but covers all of Metro Vancouver from a single base. Nobody is winning on all three dimensions in Richmond & Delta specifically.

The neighborhood pages Basecoat built are the foundation that makes that possible. The fixes above are what lets the foundation actually carry the weight.
