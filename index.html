The honest read

Strong content. Weak technical foundation. Some defects are baked into the template every microsite uses.

In plain English

  • The pages Basecoat built are good. Google likes them.
  • But the pages have small hidden errors in their underlying code that confuse Google about what the business is and where it serves.
  • "Still being indexed" only explains part of the slowness. The rest is fixable code defects that waiting won't solve.
  • A capable web team can clear most of them in a single afternoon.

Section 04

Fixes for Basecoat.

Ordered by impact. Most live in the franchisor template, so one fix lifts every territory.

Fix 01 · Brand-wide

The hidden identity code on Colour Craft's homepage has a typo.

  • In plain English: Google reads invisible code on every business website to understand what the business is, where it operates, and how to contact it. Colour Craft's homepage has the literal word "COMPANYPHONE" sitting in that code where the actual phone number should be. It was a placeholder the developer forgot to replace before launch.
  • What happens because of it: Google sees Colour Craft as a malformed business entity and won't show rich results (star ratings, phone tap, address) for the brand in search.
  • The ask: Basecoat replaces the placeholder with proper business information markup (name, address, phone, hours, service area, ratings). Roughly an hour of work.
Critical Low effort High impact Brand-wide
Fix 02 · Brand-wide

Location pages don't tell Google "this is a local painter for this city."

  • In plain English: The neighborhood pages (Steveston, Tsawwassen, King of Prussia, Chestnut Hill, etc.) read well to a human but contain none of the hidden code that tells Google "this is a painting business that serves this specific city." Without that code, Google treats them as generic blog posts.
  • What happens because of it: The pages don't compete for local pack placements or rich local search results, which is exactly the kind of search the brand should be winning.
  • The ask: Basecoat adds local business + service markup to every location page, ideally at the template level so future franchises inherit it automatically.
Critical Med effort High impact Brand-wide
Fix 03 · Brand-wide

Google doesn't have an official map of all the new pages.

  • In plain English: Every website hands Google a list of its pages (called a sitemap) so Google knows what to index. The Colour Craft main site hands Google a sitemap, but it doesn't include any of the Vancouver, Richmond-Delta, or Philadelphia microsite pages. Google has to find those pages by stumbling onto them through links instead.
  • What happens because of it: Google crawls and ranks those pages much more slowly. This is the single most likely cause behind Basecoat's "still being indexed" message.
  • The ask: Basecoat updates the main site's instructions to Google to include each microsite's sitemap, then submits each one in Google Search Console. Roughly 30 minutes of work.
Critical Low effort High impact Brand-wide
Fix 04 · Brand-wide

Backlinks pointing at the old colourcraft.ca URLs are landing on dead pages.

  • The good news: Basecoat is correctly 301-redirecting colourcraft.ca to colourcraftpainting.com for top-level paths. The homepage redirect works, and major paths work.
  • The subtle problem: Before the 2025 rebuild, Colour Craft was a single Vancouver-area painting business. The old site used a /locations/{city}/ URL structure for its Vancouver-region service areas (Squamish, Whistler, North Vancouver, West Vancouver, Burnaby, etc.). The new site uses a different structure (/vancouver/squamish/, /vancouver/north-vancouver/, etc.). The old URLs 301 to paths that don't exist on the new site, so they land on 404 errors.
  • What gets lost: Every external backlink pointing to the old /locations/* URLs (HomeStars, BBB, directories, news mentions, partner sites, anything from before the 2025 rebuild) currently lands on a 404. The SEO authority Brad's original brand built up over the years isn't transferring to the new Vancouver microsite. It's being thrown away.
  • The ask: Basecoat adds per-URL 301s mapping the old structure to the new Vancouver microsite. Example: /locations/squamish/ → /vancouver/squamish/, /locations/north-vancouver/ → /vancouver/north-vancouver/, /locations/whistler/ → /vancouver/whistler/. Basecoat will know the full list of old URLs from the previous site's sitemap. Roughly a one-hour task. Preserves every backlink the original brand earned.
High Low effort High impact Brand-wide
Fix 05 · Brand-wide

The Vancouver microsite has duplicate pages targeting the same keywords as Richmond-Delta.

  • In plain English: The Vancouver microsite has live pages at /vancouver/richmond/, /vancouver/delta/, /vancouver/tsawwassen/, and /vancouver/ladner/. The Richmond-Delta microsite has its own pages for the same cities. Both sets of pages return 200-status and each claims itself as the canonical version.
  • What happens because of it: Two pages on the same domain targeting the same keywords is called keyword cannibalization. Google has to pick one of the two to rank for "Richmond painters" and ends up either picking one and demoting the other, or splitting ranking signals so neither performs as well as a consolidated single page would. Internal authority dilutes across both URLs, and crawl budget is wasted indexing duplicate pages.
  • The ask: Basecoat 301-redirects the Vancouver-namespace overlap pages to their Richmond-Delta equivalents. /vancouver/richmond/ → /richmond-delta/richmond-bc/, /vancouver/delta/ → /richmond-delta/delta/, and same for Tsawwassen and Ladner. Vancouver keeps Squamish, Whistler, West/North/East Vancouver. Consolidates all the SEO benefit to one canonical page per territory.
High Low effort High impact Brand-wide
Fix 06 · Philadelphia

Two of GianCarlo's neighborhood pages have typos in the URL.

  • In plain English: The web address for the Radnor Township page is misspelled as /randor-township-wayne/. The King of Prussia page is misspelled as /king-of-prussias/. Both are major affluent suburbs in the Philadelphia market.
  • What happens because of it: Typos in URLs reduce click-through rate, hurt brand credibility with detail-oriented homeowners, and get harder to fix the longer they stay live (because Google starts ranking them, and changing them risks breaking those rankings).
  • The ask: Basecoat renames the pages to the correct spellings and adds redirects from the old typo URLs to the new clean ones.
High Low effort Med impact
Fix 07 · Franchisor homepage

The franchisor homepage doesn't say what Colour Craft does or where.

  • In plain English: When someone searches "Colour Craft" in Google, the result currently shows "Professional Painting Services · Colour Craft" with a description saying the brand operates "across multiple markets." No service keyword, no location, nothing compelling to click on.
  • What happens because of it: Even when the brand shows up in search, the click-through rate is hurt, and Google has less context to rank the homepage for relevant searches.
  • The ask: Basecoat rewrites the homepage title, description, and main heading to clearly state what Colour Craft does and where. Example: "House Painters Across Canada & the US · Colour Craft Painting."
High Low effort Med impact
Fix 08 · Brand-wide

Smaller wins to clean up in the same pass.

  • FAQ markup, in plain English: The location pages already contain FAQ-style content (questions and answers about pricing, process, timing). Adding a small piece of hidden code that labels it as "FAQ" lets Google display those answers directly inside search results, which significantly increases click-through.
  • Page speed, in plain English: The franchisor homepage is 722KB. The Vancouver homepage is 1.36MB. On mobile networks this slows the load, which hurts both ranking and visitor experience. Basecoat should run a speed test and identify what's causing the bloat.
  • Mobile accessibility: The site currently prevents users from pinch-zooming on mobile (a setting in the page code). That's an accessibility violation and hurts Google's mobile-experience scoring. One-line fix in the template.
  • Brand-name consistency in code: The hidden business name varies across microsites: "Colour Craft", "Colour Craft Painting", "Colour Craft Vancouver", "Colourcraft Philadelphia" (one word!). Should be standardized to one canonical brand name across every page.
  • Security headers: The site is on Cloudflare but isn't using modern security headers (HSTS, content security policy, frame protection). Cloudflare can add all of these via a single rule. Small but signals professionalism to Google.
Medium Med effort Med impact Brand-wide

Section 05

Beyond the technical fixes.

Three items that aren't direct code fixes: a franchisor brand-protection move, a coordination ask, and the franchisee-side work that runs in parallel.

Item A · Franchisor

Brand-typo domain recovery.

  • colorcraftpainting.com (American spelling) redirects to a third-party link shortener (l.ink).
  • Brand traffic from US-keyboard typing or voice search is being captured by someone outside Colour Craft.
  • Action: acquire the domain and 301-redirect it to colourcraftpainting.com, or pursue legal transfer if it appears to be cybersquatting.
Item B · Coordination

Data access for franchisees + a one-time backlink audit.

  • Search Console + GA4: Franchisees currently can't see which queries their microsites rank for or whether GA4 is configured correctly. Basecoat grants viewer access to franchisee owners. Costs nothing, unlocks data-driven decisions at every territory.
  • One-time backlink audit: Without Ahrefs or Semrush data, no one knows the brand's current backlink position. Ask Basecoat to export a one-time backlink report so you can see which sites point at colourcraft.ca vs colourcraftpainting.com, what anchor text is being used, and whether the old /locations/ URLs have meaningful equity worth recovering via Fix 04.
Item C · Franchisee

Local levers Basecoat can't pull.

  • Google Business Profile completeness (services, products, posts, Q&A, photos).
  • Review velocity. Richmond-Delta sits at 49 reviews vs. local leader at 161.
  • Citation NAP consistency across HomeStars, BBB, Yelp, Houzz.
  • Google Local Services Ads enrollment + Google Guaranteed badge.
  • Framing: Basecoat owns the on-site half. Franchisees own the off-site half. Both move in parallel.

Section 06

The leverage is in the template. Fix once at the franchisor level, every market and every future market benefits without rework.
